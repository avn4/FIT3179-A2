Can Money Buy Football Power?

European nations such as Spain and France are often ranked within the top 5 and boast GDP per capita well above the world average. This raises the question: do richer nations tend to rank higher in FIFA men's rankings? This page explores that relationship using a global map, a rank-income scatterplot, time-series trajectories, and a distribution by GDP band.

What to look for

Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to concentrate at better ranks, though outliers exist. Money helps, but we don't see a guarantee in football power. There are over-performers with better FIFA ranks and low GDP per capita (Senegal and Morocco) and under-performers (Lithuania, Malta, Hong Kong) This implies that factors like football culture, governance and talent pipelines also play a big role aside from money

Takeaways

Money improves the odds of football power. The stacked bar chart shows that wealthier countries (50k+) have more top 10 ranked countries and is far less likely to be 101+. However, it doesn't guarantee it. Many rich nations remain mid-table while some nations with lower gdp per capita have much better rankings. This suggests that investment helps, but systems, culture and talent depth are as equally important than just money.
